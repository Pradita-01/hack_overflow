Lake Size Information

This page contains detailed information about the size of the lake at Chhota Shigri Glacier, its changes over time, and the factors affecting its size.

Factors Affecting the Lake Size of the Chhota Shigri Glacier

Temperature

Temperature is one of the primary factors influencing glacier melt. Rising temperatures increase glacier melt, contributing to larger glacier-fed lakes. High temperatures can cause rapid melting of the glacier, thereby affecting the size of the lake.

Precipitation

Increased snowfall during off-seasons can contribute to the growth of the glacier, potentially reducing the lake size. However, during warmer seasons, this increased snow can lead to higher melt rates, thereby increasing the lake size. Rainfall, on the other hand, directly increases the lake size.

Glacier Dynamics

The rate of glacier movement and the formation of ice dams, which naturally store glacial water, play significant roles in determining lake size.

Ice Dam Formation

Ice dams can hold glacial water, but if the water exceeds the capacity of these natural dams, it can cause severe flooding.

Other Reasons

Several other factors, including sediment deposition, solar radiation, human activities, and global warming, also affect the lake size.
