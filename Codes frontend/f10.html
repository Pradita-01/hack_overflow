Chhota Shigri Glacier: Dam Stability

There are many factors that affect the stability of the dam.

Seismic Activity

Earthquakes and tremors cause significant damage to the natural balance of glacial dams, leading to destabilization and potential cracks.

Global Warming

The Chhota Shigri Glacier has experienced accelerated melting due to rising global temperatures. Warmer temperatures result in more melting ice, increasing water volume and putting pressure on the dam, weakening its structure. Continuous warming causes the glacier to thin, reducing its mass and making it more susceptible to collapse.

Moraine Dams Formation

As glaciers melt faster due to warming, the Chhota Shigri Glacier, like others, is at risk.

Remote Sensing and Field Studies

Remote sensing data indicates an increase in glacial lake size, adding pressure to moraine dams and increasing the risk of failure.

Sensors Used for Dam Stability

The following sensors are used to monitor dam stability:

Piezoelectric Sensor
Temperature Sensor
Inclinometer
Extensometer
Acoustic Emission

the sensors play a very important role in monitoring and reporting any issues
